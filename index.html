Portfolios

SPSS - Parametric and Nonparametric Analysis of New Born Birth Weight

Dataset containing information on new born babies and their parents were analyzed in SPSS. It was hypothesized that, the nonsmoking mothers have newborn babies on average that weigh more than the newborn babies from mothers that smoke. Independent t-test (parametric) and Mann-Whitney U test (nonparametric) analysis were conducted.

Factorial Analysis on Influence of Pop Drink Carbonation, Caffeine and Sweetner on Learning and Memory

Given the prevalence of soft drink machines on campus we are interested in finding out if consumption of these drinks had an impact on learning. We examined three variables related to the drinks: carbonation, caffeine, and sweetener. A group of students were given a novel text to read and remember while consuming the drinks. Later these students were given a recall test and the number of recall errors was recorded.

Dashboard of Ohio Covid Data

Data Cleaning, Data Joining, R Shiny App

Data of Ohio Covid was streamed daily to create a data visualization dashboard. The data was cleaned and merged to the population dataset. County population percentages was estimated. County map, time series plot, line graph, bar graph of vaccinated ohioans by age group and population estimate were included on the dashboard.
