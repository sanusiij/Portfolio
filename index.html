SPSS - Parametric and Nonparametric Analysis of New Born Birth Weight

Dataset containing information on new born babies and their parents were analyzed in SPSS. It was hypothesized that, the nonsmoking mothers have newborn babies on average that weigh more than the newborn babies from mothers that smoke. Independent t-test (parametric) and Mann-Whitney U test (nonparametric) analysis were conducted.

SAS - Association Between Diabetes And Stroke: A Longitudinal Data Analysis Among Older Americans Aged 55 and Above

In the United States, diabetes is the seventh leading cause of death and 65% of these deaths are attributable to cardiovascular diseases or stroke or to both. Stroke is the leading cause of long-term adult disability and the fifth leading cause of death in the United States, with ≈795,000 stroke events each year. The study aim to investigate the prevalence of diabetes in stroke and to examine the association of several confound variables with diabetes on stroke.

Forecasting passengers booking is necessary in order to adequately plan for staffing and resources. 144 months (12 years) booking dataset of an airline was used to forecast for the next 12 months (13th year). Stationarity of the data was checked before forecasting.
